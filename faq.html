Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions students have about research.

What is the value of research? Why should I do it?

Research can open your eyes to new career opportunities and help you gain experience in the scientific field. Although it is not the most important aspect, research will be very beneficial in your college application process and when you search for scholarships. Research is one of the few opportunities students can do to change the world. Also, sometimes there is compensation.

Is there a minimum age to do research in a lab?

Generally, a student needs to be at least 15. Different research programs and labs may have an alternative age minimum.

There is a pandemic currently. How can I participate in research when institutions are in lockdown?

There are two types of research: wet and dry. Wet lab essentially consists of performing experiments in a laboratory. Dry lab consists of doing research outside of the lab, like at home. The subjects that are dry lab are generally computer science related or more of the humanities. Dry lab will probably be your best bet during a pandemic.

I really want to participate in research, but I am scared I am not knowledgeable enough in the field. What should I do?

Don't worry! Professors and other researchers do not expect you to know very much when you begin. Just make sure to be open-minded and give it your all!

I live very far away from any research institution. Is there a way for me to conduct research still?

Yes! Dry lab is probably your best option, since dry lab can be done anywhere.

Scientific papers are really difficult to read, how can I efficiently read and understand these papers?

Scientific papers are really difficult to read initially, but check out our "How to read papers" page to understand the techniques you can use to approach reading scientific papers.
